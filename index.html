Savitribai Phule

The first female teacher of India and a pioneer of women's education.

About Timeline

Who is Savitribai Phule?

Savitribai Phule was the first female teacher of India. She was born in 1831 and worked hard to give education to girls during a time when it was not allowed. Along with her husband, Jyotirao Phule, she started the first school for girls in Pune. She also fought against child marriage, caste discrimination, and unfair treatment of women. Savitribai is remembered as a brave social reformer who changed many lives.

Achievements of Savitribai Phule

  • India’s First Female Teacher – She became the first woman teacher in India in 1848.
  • Started the First Girls’ School – Along with her husband Jyotirao Phule, she opened the first school for girls in Pune.
  • Promoted Women’s Education – She worked fearlessly to educate girls, Dalits, and those from backward castes.
  • Fought Against Social Evils – She raised her voice against child marriage, untouchability, sati, and gender discrimination.
  • Started a Shelter for Widows – She opened a home for pregnant widows and helped them raise their children with respect and safety.
  • Published Inspiring Poetry – She was a poet who wrote powerful poems on women’s rights and social justice.
  • Broke Caste Barriers – She taught children from all castes, breaking strong social rules of that time.
  • Led During Plague Epidemic – During the 1897 plague outbreak, she helped sick people and unfortunately died while serving them.

Timeline

  • January 3, 1831 – Born in Naigaon, Maharashtra.
  • 1840 – Married Jyotirao Phule at the age of 9.
  • 1848 – Became the first female teacher in India and opened the first girls' school in Pune with her husband.
  • 1852 – Helped open three more schools for girls and backward castes.
  • 1854 – Started a home for widows and pregnant women with no support.
  • 1858 – Adopted a child of a Brahmin widow, Yashwantrao, challenging social norms.
  • 1873 – Co-founded the Satyashodhak Samaj, a group fighting for equality and social justice.
  • 1890 – Her husband, Jyotirao Phule, passed away; she continued his mission.
  • 1897 – Worked during the plague epidemic in Pune, helping infected people.
  • March 10, 1897 – Passed away due to infection while serving plague victims.
